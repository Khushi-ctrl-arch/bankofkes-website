Letter of Credit:

1.Agreement :

The buyer and seller agree to use a Letter of Credit as the method of payment in their transaction.

2.Issuance:

The buyer (applicant) applies to their bank (issuing bank) to issue an LC in favor of the seller (beneficiary).

3.Application:

The buyer submits necessary documents and information to the issuing bank, including details of the transaction, terms and conditions, and specific instructions.

4.Issuance by Issuing Bank:

The issuing bank reviews the application and, if everything is in order, issues the LC. This is a formal commitment to pay the beneficiary (seller) upon presentation of compliant documents.

5.Transmission:

The issuing bank reviews the application and, if everything is in order, issues the LC. This is a formal commitment to pay the beneficiary (seller) upon presentation of compliant documents.

6.Advice to Beneficiary:

The advising bank notifies the beneficiary (seller) that an LC has been issued in their favor.

7.Shipment and Documentation:

The seller ships the goods and gathers the required documents specified in the LC (such as bill of lading, invoice, certificate of origin).

8.Presentation:

The seller presents the required documents to the advising bank.

9.Examination:

The advising bank checks whether the documents comply with the terms and conditions of the LC

10.Payment/Reimbursement:

If the documents are in order, the advising bank forwards them to the issuing bank, which checks them again. If everything is correct, the issuing bank honors the LC by effecting payment to the advising bank or reimbursing the negotiating bank (if a negotiation was involved).

11.Dispute Resolution:

If there are discrepancies in the documents, the banks may negotiate or the buyer and seller might need to resolve the issues.
