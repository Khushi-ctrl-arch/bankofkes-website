Cash Withdrawal:

Steps:

1.Prepare Identification:

Ensure you have a valid identification document such as a passport, driver's license, or national ID card. Some banks may also require your bank card (debit or ATM card).

2.Visit the Bank:

Go to your bank branch where you have your account. If you're using an ATM, locate a nearby machine affiliated with your bank.

3.Queue (if necessary):

If there's a queue, wait for your turn. Be prepared to show your identification and bank card if required.

4.Approach the Teller or ATM:

If using a teller:Approach the teller window.

5.Verify Your Identity:

The teller may ask you security questions or for additional identification to confirm your identity.

6.Request the Withdrawal:

State the amount of cash you want to withdraw.

7.Receive and Count the Cash:

Once the transaction is processed, the teller will hand you the cash. Count it to ensure it matches the amount you requested.

8.Collect Receipt (if provided):

The teller will usually provide a receipt for your transaction. Keep this for your records.

If using ATM:

1.Insert your bank card into the ATM.
2.Enter your PIN (Personal Identification Number).
3.Select "Withdrawal" from the options provided.
4.Enter the amount you want to withdraw.
5.Collect your cash and receipt (if provided).
6.Secure Your Cash: Immediately store the cash securely, such as in your wallet or purse.
7.Receive Confirmation: Once approved, you'll receive confirmation of your new savings account. This may include account details, how to access online banking, and any related materials (e.g., debit card).
8.Manage Your Account: Start using your savings account to deposit money, earn interest, and save for your financial goals.
